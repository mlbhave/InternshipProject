ABOUT

What Is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting devices, networks, and data from unauthorized access, damage, or theft. As more of our lives move online — banking, shopping, education, and work — staying safe digitally is no longer optional. A single careless click can expose personal data, drain a bank account, or lock you out of years of work. Awareness is the cheapest, most effective defense anyone can build.

Confidentiality

Keeping sensitive information private and accessible only to people who are authorized to see it.

Integrity

Making sure data isn't altered, corrupted, or tampered with — what you saved is what you get back.

Availability

Ensuring systems and information are accessible to the right people exactly when they're needed.

The Human Firewall

Most breaches start with a person, not a machine. An informed user is the strongest layer of defense.

KNOW YOUR ENEMY

Common Cyber Threats

Understanding how an attack works is the first step toward stopping it. Here are five threats every internet user should be able to recognize.

Malware

Malicious software designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to a device — including viruses, worms, and trojans hidden inside downloads or attachments.

Prevention Tips

  • Only download apps and files from trusted sources
  • Keep antivirus software active and updated
  • Avoid pirated software and unknown USB drives

Phishing

Deceptive emails, texts, or websites that impersonate trusted brands or people to trick you into revealing passwords, card numbers, or other personal data.

Prevention Tips

  • Never click links from unexpected or urgent messages
  • Check the sender's email address carefully
  • Go directly to the official website instead of clicking

Ransomware

A type of malware that encrypts your files and demands payment — usually in cryptocurrency — to restore access. There's no guarantee paying gets your data back.

Prevention Tips

  • Back up important files regularly, offline if possible
  • Don't open attachments from unknown senders
  • Patch your operating system and apps promptly

Spyware

Software that secretly monitors your activity — keystrokes, browsing habits, even camera and microphone access — and sends that data to an attacker without your knowledge.

Prevention Tips

  • Review app permissions before installing
  • Use reputable anti-spyware / antivirus tools
  • Avoid clicking pop-ups and unverified ad links

Weak Passwords

Short, reused, or predictable passwords are one of the easiest entry points for attackers using automated guessing tools or leaked password databases.

Prevention Tips

  • Use a unique password for every account
  • Aim for 12+ characters mixing case, numbers, symbols
  • Use a password manager instead of memorizing or reusing

Social Engineering

Psychological manipulation that pressures people into breaking normal security procedure — posing as IT support, a coworker, or an authority figure.

Prevention Tips

  • Verify identity through a separate, known channel
  • Be suspicious of urgency, secrecy, or pressure
  • Follow your organization's verification process

    Spot the Phishing Attempt

    Phishing attacks copy real brands almost perfectly. Train your eye to spot the small details that give them away.

    Suspicious Email

    From: support@secure-paypa1-alerts.com

    Subject: URGENT: Your account will be suspended in 24 hours!

    Dear Customer, we detected unusual activity. Click below immediately to verify your identity or your account will be permanently locked.

    Verify My Account Now →
    • Misspelled domain ("paypa1")
    • Generic "Dear Customer" greeting
    • Manufactured urgency / threat
    Legitimate Email

    From: service@paypal.com

    Subject: Your monthly statement is ready

    Hi Asha, your account statement for June is now available. Log in to your account directly to view your transaction history.

    Log In at paypal.com →
    • Correct, verified sender domain
    • Personalized greeting
    • No pressure or threats
    Suspicious Website
    http://paypa1-secure-login.com
    Sign in to confirm your billing details to avoid suspension.
    • No HTTPS / open padlock
    • Look-alike domain spelling
    • Uses "http" instead of secure "https"
    Legitimate Website
    https://www.paypal.com
    Welcome back! Log in to view your balance and transactions.
    • Secure HTTPS connection (padlock)
    • Exact, correctly spelled domain
    • No urgent demands on landing

    Universal Red Flags

    Urgent or threatening language
    Generic, impersonal greetings
    Mismatched or odd links / domains
    Requests for money or credentials
    Unexpected attachments
    Offers that seem too good to be true
    Spelling and grammar mistakes
    Pressure to act immediately
